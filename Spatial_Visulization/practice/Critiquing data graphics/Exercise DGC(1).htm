Exercise DGC / Critiquing data graphics

Objectives

  1. To gain experience of the different types of data graphics commonly encountered;  
  2. To critically assess data graphics; 
  3. To practice your presentation skills and team work. 

In a nutshell...

Your task is, in your groups, to seek out a variety of interesting or thought-provoking data graphics, and critically analyze these. You will report back to the class by sharing your data results on your group blog and presenting to the class the best and worst instance you found, explaining your analysis to the class, and be prepared to answer questions on your chosen instances. 

The fine print...

You should complete this exercise in your groups of 3 or 4 people. You can split your task into three stages.

Stage 1: Collect and collate a corpus of data graphics

Use your resourcefulness to find and survey a small corpus of data graphics. You may find it useful to visit the library, to read the papers, browse the magazines in your local newsagent, or simply look around your home or classroom, as well as of course search the Internet.

Stage 2: Critically analyze your corpus 

Critically assess your corpus, using the visual variables (and related topics) covered in lectures so far. From your analysis, identify the one of the best and one of the worst data graphics in your corpus, and refine your analysis as necessary.  

Stage 3: Share your findings 

You must upload links to a selection of your best/worst graphics to your group Blog, along with a brief (<100 word) explanation of your analysis. 

Selected groups must also orally present findings to the whole class. Each group will have at most 4 minutes briefly to present either their best or worst interface/graphics and to summarize their analysis and justifications for the choice. You will be able to access your graphic/interface via the Wiki (no powerpoint, no bullet points). You should be prepared to answer questions on your choices and analysis, as well as support other presenters by asking questions about their choices.

At the end, we shall hold a class vote on the overall best and worst interface and graphic.

Assessment

This exercise is not assessed.
