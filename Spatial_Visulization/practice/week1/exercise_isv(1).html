Exercise ISV / Introduction to spatial visualization 

Objectives

  1. To gain exposure to a wide variety of styles of presentation and interaction with spatial information;  
  2. To explore your opinions on these spatial "visualizations" and begin to identify the reasons behind those opinions; 
  3. To listen critically to other's opinions on spatial visualizations. 

In a nutshell...

Your task is survey different types of "spatial visualizations" you can find on the Web, and report back to the class about an example that particularly interests you.  

The fine print...

You should complete this exercise in groups of 3 or 4 students. You may decide on your groups amongst yourselves, or else you will be allocated to a group. Many of the practicals will operate in groups, so you will have a chance to get to know your group members over the coming two weeks.

You can split your task into two stages.

Stage 1: Web search and analysis

Search the Web for examples of as many different "spatial visualizations" as you can find. These might include maps, animations, GIS interfaces, graphics from news articles or blogs, or indeed any form of presentation of geographic information (images, videos, software, etc.). One example to start you off is the Empires Decline Revisited* by Pedro Miguel Cruz, http://vimeo.com/11506746. As you find more, try to identify similar and dissimilar examples, construct a tentative classification of types of spatial visualization. 

Stage 2: Share and report back on your results

You should upload links to the best examples you find in your group blog site. Select one of your examples which you particularly like (or dislike) and prepare to briefly present that example to the class, explaining what it is about your example you (dis)like. You should be ready to answer questions about your reasons. Have a look at other groups blog sites, and feel free to give (constructive) comments and feedback on your own views of the examples others post.

Assessment

This exercise is not assessed.
